# RESEARCH — probing fuel

Angles that are easy to under-weight when the question is settled by external evidence — technology evaluation, library or vendor choice, "what's the state of X". Considerations, not an agenda — most won't apply. Press the load-bearing ones; priority stays yours.

## Blind-spot probes
- **Seller vs operator** — Does the load-bearing claim come from someone selling the thing? Find what someone who runs it in production says, especially about the parts the marketing skips.
- **Survivorship** — Success stories get written; abandonments go quiet. Who adopted this and then left — and what drove them out?
- **Claim decay** — Claims about fast-moving things rot. Which version or date does each load-bearing claim describe, and is that still the current reality?
- **Issue tracker over README** — The README says what it should do; the issue tracker says what it does. Checked the open issues against your exact use case?
- **Popularity vs fit** — Stars, downloads, and adoption measure other people's problems. What's the actual evidence under your constraints — scale, team, ecosystem?
- **Informed dissent** — Every real option has competent detractors. Found them? Their reasons are usually the trade-offs the favorable sources didn't mention.

## Forced trade-offs
- Breadth vs depth — survey more candidates shallowly or fewer deeply; which does this decision actually need?
- Read more vs try it — is a small spike or prototype cheaper and more decisive than another round of sources?
